Chow Mein

Description

Chow Mein is a classic Chinese stir-fried noodle dish that is popular worldwide. The name "Chow Mein" translates to "fried noodles" in Cantonese, reflecting the dish's cooking method. Chow Mein typically consists of stir-fried noodles, vegetables, and meat (such as chicken, beef, or pork), all seasoned with a savory sauce. It's a versatile and flavorful dish that can be customized according to personal preference.


Difficulty: Easy

Time to Make: 20 minutes

Ingredients

  • 200g chow mein noodles
  • 1 chicken breast, thinly sliced
  • 2 cups mixed vegetables (such as bell peppers, carrots, cabbage, and bean sprouts)
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon ginger, minced
  • 2 green onions, sliced
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon oyster sauce
  • 1 teaspoon sesame oil
  • 1 teaspoon sugar
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Steps:

  1. Cook the chow mein noodles according to the package instructions. Drain and set aside.
  2. Heat vegetable oil in a wok or large skillet over medium-high heat. Add the sliced chicken breast and stir-fry until cooked through. Remove from the wok and set aside.
  3. In the same wok, add a little more oil if needed. Add the minced garlic and ginger, and stir-fry until fragrant.
  4. Add the mixed vegetables to the wok and stir-fry until they are tender-crisp.
  5. Return the cooked chicken to the wok. Add the cooked chow mein noodles, soy sauce, oyster sauce, sesame oil, and sugar. Toss everything together until well combined and heated through.
  6. Season with salt and pepper to taste. Garnish with sliced green onions before serving.
