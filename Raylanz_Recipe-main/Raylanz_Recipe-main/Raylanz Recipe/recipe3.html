Peking Roast Duck

Description

Created for the Ming Dynasty, Peking roast duck has since become a common delicacy enjoyed in Beijing, China. Seasoned before being oven-roasted, Peking duck is often served right out of the oven with its signature crisp and golden-brazed skin still intact. Served with the duck, bringing the dish together, is sides of spring onion, cucumber, and sweet bean sauce.

Difficulty: Advanced

Time to Prepare: 24 hours (including marination and drying time)

Ingredients

  • 1 whole duck, cleaned and pat dried
  • 1/4 cup soy sauce
  • 2 tablespoons honey
  • 1 tablespoon five-spice powder
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1 tablespoon vegetable oil

Steps:

  1. In a bowl, mix soy sauce, honey, five-spice powder, salt, and black pepper.
  2. Brush the duck inside and out with the sauce mixture.
  3. Refrigerate the duck uncovered for at least 6 hours or overnight.
  4. Preheat the oven to 350°F (175°C).
  5. Place the duck on a rack in a roasting pan and roast for 2 to 2.5 hours, basting occasionally with the remaining sauce.
  6. Let the duck rest for 10 minutes before carving.
  7. Serve hot with steamed pancakes, hoisin sauce, sliced scallions, and cucumber.
